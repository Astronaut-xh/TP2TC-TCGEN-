Workplace Childhood Friends: A New Bond
China Daily | social_life

In China's high-pressure labor market, a new term is gaining popularity among young professionals: "workplace childhood friends." This refers to the special bond formed between entry-level hires who join a company at the same time. Unlike the strategic networking common among senior managers, these relationships grow through shared struggles during the difficult transition from school to work, when early professional failures and steep learning curves bring people together.

Moreover, real-life examples show how powerful these bonds can be. Chen Xinyi, a banking professional in Xi'an, was sent to Beijing for six months of intensive training along with about 50 new recruits. Living together in a hotel and navigating a new city, they built deep trust. When Chen suffered a personal crisis, a colleague silently took over her workload and comforted her without asking questions. Similarly, Tian Xiao, a former TV news editor, developed a "silent rhythm" of mutual support with his partner during 12-hour night shifts in the pandemic. Even after leaving the company, their bond continues through a WeChat group where members share life updates and photos of their children.

In addition, these friendships serve different roles across professions. In the civil service, they act as a safeguard against professional isolation, while in the technology sector, some young workers even bond over shared personality types, creating understanding so deep that they can cancel social plans at the last minute without explanation.

Furthermore, as China's workplace culture gradually moves away from the "lone wolf" model, the friends made at the starting line of a career have become essential emotional support for a generation facing high job mobility. With hashtags related to "first-job camaraderie" surging on social media, it is clear that emotional connection is now valued just as highly as career advancement itself.

From Shoulder Poles to Global Tables: The Rise of Shaxian Snacks
China Daily | culture_education

In Yubang village, Fujian province, Zhang Xiuji plunges wontons into boiling water and tosses noodles with practiced ease. After years of running snack shops across China, she has finally returned home to a steady local job. The Shaxian snack tradition she learned from her mother comprises over 200 varieties, with signature dishes such as bianrou — wontons with a springy meat filling — and banmian — noodles topped with savory peanut sauce. A local saying captures how villagers built their prosperity: wontons are the "bricks" and noodles are the "steel" that constructed the region's modern skyline.

Zhang's personal journey mirrors the brand's remarkable rise. In the late 1990s, burdened by 200,000 yuan in debt, she and her husband set out with nothing but wooden mallets and shoulder poles to make a living in Fuzhou, eventually expanding their business to Shanghai and Jiangsu province. Today, Shaxian Delicacies has grown into a national brand with 100,000 outlets across China and an annual turnover reaching 55 billion yuan in 2025.

The brand has also expanded globally, with 241 overseas branches spanning 81 countries. These stores adapt creatively to local tastes: in Saudi Arabia, pork-filled wontons are reimagined to respect local dietary customs; in Japan, chefs apply peanut sauce more generously; and in France, a drizzle of salad oil adds a touch of local flair. Meanwhile, the rise of foodie tourism is drawing former migrants back to Yubang. With standardized ingredients now delivered by smartphone order, Zhang reflects: "The hardships we once endured are now a thing of the past."

Fengyun-4C: China's Most Capable Weather Satellite Strengthens Disaster Defense
China Daily | science_technology

The China Meteorological Administration has released the first images from the newly launched Fengyun-4C satellite, demonstrating the nation's capability to monitor conditions from the sun to Earth in near real time. Launched on Dec 27 from the Xichang Satellite Launch Center, the satellite began its in-orbit tests in early January, and it is currently the world's most capable single geostationary meteorological satellite in orbit in terms of comprehensive observation capabilities, according to the administration. The deployment of the Fengyun-4C follows the central government's policy directives for more robust monitoring of extreme weather conditions and stronger disaster prevention efforts, aiming to strengthen the first line of defense against meteorological disasters and expected to enter its operational trial phase before this year's primary flood season.

The satellite carries six payloads meeting advanced international standards, with its geostationary radiation imager having produced detailed animations of short-wave trough cloud systems, enabling forecasters to distinguish high-level cirrus clouds with unprecedented clarity. Meanwhile, an interference-based vertical atmospheric sounder provided refined spectral data, capturing more temperature layers than previous models, which improves the accuracy of global numerical weather prediction models. Additionally, a lightning imager mapped convective activity over South Asia, tracking storm cells and lightning events across Bangladesh and Myanmar.

Beyond Earth observation, new ultraviolet and X-ray sensors captured solar flares and ionospheric glow phenomena, helping mitigate the impact of solar activity on global communication, navigation and positioning systems. The Fengyun-4C is set to become a primary data source for MAZU, China's AI-powered early warning system, and upon reaching full service will support international efforts in global monitoring, forecasting and environmental governance. Chen Zhenlin, director of the China Meteorological Administration, noted that China has built the world's largest and most comprehensive meteorological observation system, with 10 Fengyun weather satellites currently in orbit, making it the only country with meteorological satellites covering four near-Earth orbital types.

Policy Push for Better Children's Medicines
China Daily | health_environment

China has issued a joint guideline aimed at improving access to pediatric drugs by boosting research and development while strengthening supply chain stability. Although demand for medicines targeting common childhood diseases can largely be met at present, the National Health Commission acknowledges a persistent shortage in the overall number of pediatric drugs, including child-appropriate dosage forms and strengths. Many drug labels also provide insufficient or substandard information on pediatric use.

Pharmaceutical companies remain inadequately incentivized to develop and produce pediatric drugs, making the supply of certain low-volume but clinically essential children's medicines persistently unreliable. To mitigate these challenges, the guideline calls for refining supporting policies for the priority pediatric drug catalog and granting listed products accelerated review and approval pathways, while also giving such drugs preferential inclusion in the national essential medicines list, major state-level R&D programs, and the national drug reimbursement list. Drug regulators will enhance early communication with developers and support R&D efficiency through rolling data submissions.

The guideline further supports establishing pediatric research wards at major medical centers and exploring the construction of a national pediatric clinical trial network alongside a cross-institutional ethics review mechanism to pool resources and coordinate participant recruitment. Healthcare institutions will be guided to conduct joint research on drugs suitable for children but lacking pediatric labeling, allowing safety data from Chinese adults to be extrapolated to pediatric populations.

To strengthen supply, more pediatric medicines will be added to centralized production bases dedicated to manufacturing drugs in short supply, while monitoring of production and specialized materials will be intensified. During seasonal infectious disease outbreaks, coordinated early warning mechanisms will be improved with reinforced supplies of antivirals and pediatric medicines for relieving fever and pain. Commercial healthcare insurers will also be encouraged to develop pediatric insurance products covering innovative drugs and rare disease medications.

易错点
• 绝对化陷阱：将原文的辅助性措施理解为完全替代方案。如Q2中A选项将“允许数据外推“曲解为“完全替代所有儿科临床试验“，原文用allowing表明这只是补充性手段，且同时还在建立临床试验网络。（Q1）💡 看到allowing、support、encourage等表示“辅助/鼓励“的词时，要警惕选项中出现replace、entirely、eliminate等绝对化表述，二者逻辑上互斥。
• 无中生有陷阱：选项引入原文完全未提及的信息。如Q1中C选项“独占专利保护延期“在原文中毫无依据，命题者利用考生将“优惠政策“与”专利保护“主观关联的心理设置干扰。（Q1）💡 做细节题时，每个选项必须在原文中找到明确对应。如果某个选项的关键概念（如patent、extension）在原文中完全找不到，即使看起来“合理“也应排除。
• 范围扩大陷阱：将原文限定范围内的措施扩大到全领域。如Q2中C选项将针对“缺乏儿童标签的药物“的数据外推扩大为“所有未来儿科药物审批的首要方法“，既扩大适用范围又拔高方法地位。（Q1）💡 注意原文中的限定词（certain、specific、some等），当选项出现all、every、primary、universal等泛化词时，需回到原文核实范围是否被偷换。
• 立场反转陷阱：将作者支持的态度误判为质疑或批评。如Q3中A选项Skeptical和D选项Critical与全文建设性、肯定性的语调完全相反。（Q1）💡 判断态度题时，留意全文的措辞倾向。如果文章系统性阐述政策如何应对问题，无任何质疑或保留表述，则作者态度为支持性而非批判性。
